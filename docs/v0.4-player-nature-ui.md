# Dyna v0.4 — Player Nature UI Notes

This note records the first implemented slice of the `0.4 — Player as force of nature` roadmap phase.

## What changed

- Added a separate player-facing nature UI script:
  - `scripts/ui/player_nature_ui.gd`
- The player now has nature energy:
  - starts at `0`
  - maximum is `500`
  - regenerates at `+1` energy per second
- The energy bar is shown in the top-right HUD as `Энергия природы`.
- Lightning was moved out of creature stats UI and into player nature UI.
- Lightning now costs `50` energy.
- Lightning button text shows the cost: `Молния (50)`.
- Lightning button is disabled while current energy is below the spell cost.
- Energy is spent only after a successful lightning hit on a creature.

## File ownership

### `scripts/ui/player_nature_ui.gd`
Owns player-as-nature UI and nature-power state:

- player energy value;
- max energy;
- energy regeneration;
- lightning button;
- lightning targeting mode;
- lightning energy cost;
- lightning damage;
- future rain and other nature powers.

### `scripts/ui/creature_stats_ui.gd`
Should stay focused on creature observation and debug UI:

- hover preview;
- click-to-pin selection;
- creature stats panel;
- health/hunger/age display;
- FPS/debug status text;
- simulation speed selector.

It should not own player power economy. It currently keeps only a small compatibility bridge so creature click input can route lightning targeting to `player_nature_ui.gd`.

### `scenes/main/main.tscn`
Owns scene placement for the top-level UI:

- `UI/PlayerNaturePanel` uses `scripts/ui/player_nature_ui.gd`;
- the energy bar and lightning button live under this panel;
- the old standalone lightning panel is no longer needed.

## Design rule preserved

Player nature powers must remain indirect world influence, not direct unit control. Lightning is a player action, but creatures still remain autonomous simulation actors.

## Next likely v0.4 steps

- Add rain as a second nature power.
- Make rain affect grass growth/spread instead of creatures directly.
- Keep nature power costs configurable through `player_nature_ui.gd` exports.
- Consider moving player nature state out of UI later if it grows beyond simple prototype scope.
